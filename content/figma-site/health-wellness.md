## Wellness Guides

### Regular Check-ups
Annual vet visits are crucial for early detection of health issues. Adult dogs should see a vet at least once a year, while senior dogs (7+) should go twice yearly.

**Key Tips:**
- Schedule appointments in advance
- Keep vaccination records updated
- Discuss preventive care options
- Ask about breed-specific health concerns

### Nutrition & Diet
Proper nutrition is the foundation of your dog's health. Quality food appropriate for their age, size, and activity level makes all the difference.

**Key Tips:**
- Choose high-quality protein sources
- Avoid foods toxic to dogs (chocolate, grapes, onions)
- Monitor portion sizes to prevent obesity
- Fresh water should always be available

### Exercise & Activity
Regular exercise keeps your dog physically fit and mentally stimulated. Activity needs vary by breed, age, and health status.

**Key Tips:**
- Daily walks are essential for most breeds
- Mix up activities: fetch, swimming, hiking
- Mental stimulation is as important as physical
- Adjust exercise intensity for age and health

### Mental Health
Dogs experience anxiety, stress, and depression just like humans. Recognizing signs and providing emotional support is part of being a great dog dad.

**Key Tips:**
- Watch for behavioral changes
- Maintain consistent routines
- Provide enrichment activities and toys
- Consider professional help if needed

## Questions to Ask When Choosing a Vet

1. **What are your office hours and emergency coverage?** — Knowing when you can access care and what happens after hours is crucial for emergencies.
2. **What services do you offer in-house?** — Understanding what diagnostic tests, surgeries, and treatments are available on-site vs. requiring referral.
3. **Do you have experience with my dog's breed?** — Different breeds have specific health concerns that experienced vets will be familiar with.
4. **What is your approach to preventive care?** — A good vet emphasizes prevention and will create a wellness plan tailored to your dog's needs.
5. **How do you handle payment and pet insurance?** — Understanding costs upfront and knowing which insurance plans are accepted helps avoid surprises.
6. **Can I tour the facility?** — Seeing where your dog will receive care helps you assess cleanliness, equipment, and overall environment.
